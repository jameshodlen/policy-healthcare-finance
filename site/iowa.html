Iowa: MercyOne’s 755 Payers Set the Midwest Standard

94.4% median — MercyOne and UnityPoint cover the state with very different approaches.

31 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

Iowa’s 94.4% median transparency ranks among the highest in the Midwest once non-Iowa entries are cleaned from the data. MercyOne achieves 94–99% across 13 facilities with an extraordinary 755+ payer contracts each. UnityPoint clusters at 51–75% with a uniform 20% cash discount.

Key Findings

  1. MercyOne is Iowa's transparency leader: 13 facilities at 94–99.5% transparency, each disclosing rates for 752–760 payers. No other Midwest system comes close to this payer breadth.
  2. UnityPoint Health (14 facilities) clusters in the Substantial tier at 51–75% — all on v3.0.0 schema with a uniform 20% cash discount. Centralized pricing, incomplete disclosure.
  3. University of Iowa Health Care hits 100% across both campuses — the state's flagship academic center complies fully.
  4. Iowa Specialty Hospital (Clarion + Belmond) both achieve 100% with 12–15 payers and a 40% cash discount — proof that small hospitals can fully comply.
  5. MercyOne's cash discount variance spans 9–36% across facilities — compared to UnityPoint's uniform 20%. MercyOne appears to set pricing at the facility level; UnityPoint centralizes it.
  6. 14 hospitals scored 0% — mostly non-Iowa facilities from national cms-hpt.txt files (Gundersen, Trinity, Genesis) that were incorrectly attributed to Iowa in discovery.

About Transparency Tiers

Full ≥80% of items have payer-specific negotiated rates. Substantial 50–80%. Partial 20–50%. Minimal <20%. Transparency percentage measures the share of data rows containing a payer-specific negotiated rate — not just gross charges or cash prices.
