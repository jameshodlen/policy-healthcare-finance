Minnesota: The HealthPartners Contradiction

A consumer-governed nonprofit that hides what insurance actually pays.

31 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

HealthPartners — both an insurer and a hospital system — scores 6.9% at Regions Hospital. They give patients generous cash discounts (53–75% off gross) while hiding what insured patients' carriers actually negotiate.

Key Findings

  1. HealthPartners Regions Hospital — Level I Trauma, 454 beds, consumer-governed nonprofit — discloses payer rates on 6.9% of rows. The lowest non-zero score in the three-state dataset.
  2. All four HealthPartners facilities score below 12% — the only system in either state entirely below 20%. Yet their cash discounts are the highest in Minnesota (53–75%).
  3. Mayo Clinic Rochester discloses rates for 1,102 payer/plan combinations — 10x more than any other hospital in any state. The richest single chargemaster file in the dataset.
  4. Allina Health achieves 99.8–100% across all 5 analyzed facilities — proving full disclosure is operationally feasible for a large multi-hospital system.
  5. Essentia Brainerd (St. Joseph's Medical Center) scores 99.6% with 39 payers — one of the most transparent hospitals in the dataset and the strongest local data anchor.
  6. M Health Fairview shows good percentage transparency (70–90%) but only 2–4 payers per facility — the structure has rates but for very few contracts.

About Transparency Tiers

Full ≥80% of items have payer-specific negotiated rates. Substantial 50–80%. Partial 20–50%. Minimal <20%. Transparency percentage measures the share of data rows containing a payer-specific negotiated rate — not just gross charges or cash prices.
