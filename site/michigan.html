Michigan: McLaren’s Perfect Score, Corewell’s Black Box

12 McLaren hospitals at 100%. 19 Corewell hospitals at 0%. Same state, opposite choices.

55 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

Michigan is the Midwest’s starkest transparency divide. McLaren Health Care achieves 100% at all 12 facilities with 22–38 payers and a uniform 50% cash discount. Corewell Health — Michigan’s largest system (formerly Beaumont/Spectrum) — registers 0% across all 19 facilities despite listing 14–21 payers in their files.

Key Findings

  1. McLaren Health Care achieves 100% transparency at all 12 facilities — 22–38 payers, uniform 50% cash discount. The gold standard for Michigan.
  2. Corewell Health (19 facilities, Michigan’s largest) scores 0% everywhere despite payer names appearing in files. Structural non-disclosure from the state’s dominant system.
  3. Henry Ford spans the full range: Providence Novi/Southfield at 91% (Full), flagship Detroit at 44% (Partial), Kingswood behavioral at 0.1%. Wide intra-system variance.
  4. Karmanos Cancer Institute hits 100% with 32 payers — full compliance from a specialty cancer center.
  5. Munson Healthcare (8 facilities in northern MI) clusters at 45–85% with only 2–3 payers each — good rates but very few payers disclosed.
  6. Corewell’s 0% is the biggest finding — a 19-hospital system serving metro Detroit and Grand Rapids that technically publishes files but discloses no actual negotiated rates.

About Transparency Tiers

Full ≥80% of items have payer-specific negotiated rates. Substantial 50–80%. Partial 20–50%. Minimal <20%. Transparency percentage measures the share of data rows containing a payer-specific negotiated rate — not just gross charges or cash prices.
