Indiana: Deaconess Discloses, Parkview Goes Dark

Only 19 of 148 hospitals discoverable — a state-wide transparency gap.

19 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

Indiana’s chargemaster landscape is sparse. Parkview Health — Fort Wayne’s dominant system — publishes files our profiler cannot parse (0% across 10 facilities). Deaconess in Evansville is the bright spot, clustering at 54–74% with generous cash discounts of 41–70%.

Key Findings

  1. Parkview Health (10 facilities) returns 0% transparency across the board — files exist but the format blocks automated profiling.
  2. Deaconess is Indiana’s most transparent system: 6 facilities at 54–74% (Substantial tier) with 7–20 payers and cash discounts of 41–70%.
  3. Stones Crossing Imaging Center hits 98.9% with 51 payers — the only Full-tier facility in the state.
  4. 129 of 148 hospitals in the CMS inventory had no discoverable cms-hpt.txt file — the largest discovery gap in the Midwest.
  5. Indiana lacks the mega-system transparency leaders seen in neighboring states (ThedaCare in WI, MercyOne in IA, McLaren in MI).
  6. Mercy Hospital Columbus discloses 162 payers but only 25.7% transparency — many payers listed, few rates actually disclosed.

About Transparency Tiers

Full ≥80% of items have payer-specific negotiated rates. Substantial 50–80%. Partial 20–50%. Minimal <20%. Transparency percentage measures the share of data rows containing a payer-specific negotiated rate — not just gross charges or cash prices.
