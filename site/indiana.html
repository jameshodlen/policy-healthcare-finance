Indiana: IU Health’s Perfect Score Transforms the State

16 IU Health facilities at 100%. Parkview’s 10 facilities at 1–4%. Two Indianas.

31 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

IU Health — Indiana’s largest system — achieves 100% transparency at all 16 facilities with 20–29 payers and cash discounts of 53–79%. Parkview Health in Fort Wayne scores 0.5–4.4% across 10 facilities — files are JSON disguised as CSV, and what parses contains almost no negotiated rates.

Key Findings

  1. IU Health achieves 100% transparency at all 16 facilities — 20–29 payers, 53–79% cash discounts. Indiana’s gold standard and one of the best system-wide performances in the Midwest.
  2. Parkview Health (10 Fort Wayne-area facilities) scores 0.5–4.4% — files are JSON served as CSV, and what parses contains almost no negotiated rates. Minimal compliance.
  3. Deaconess (Evansville) clusters at 54–74% (Substantial) with 7–20 payers and generous cash discounts of 41–70%.
  4. Riley Children’s Hospital at IU Health hits 100% with 29 payers — full compliance at Indiana’s flagship pediatric center.
  5. IU Health’s cash discounts (53–79%) are among the highest in the Midwest — a self-pay patient at IU Health Bedford saves 70% off gross charges.
  6. Community Health Network — despite 17 hospitals in CMS inventory — only Stones Crossing Imaging Center was discoverable (100%, 51 payers).

About Transparency Tiers

Full ≥80% of items have payer-specific negotiated rates. Substantial 50–80%. Partial 20–50%. Minimal <20%. Transparency percentage measures the share of data rows containing a payer-specific negotiated rate — not just gross charges or cash prices.
