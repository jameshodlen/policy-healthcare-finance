Nebraska: The Midwest’s Quiet Transparency Leader

79.1% median — the highest in the Midwest, with zero hospitals at 0%.

19 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

Nebraska has the highest median transparency in the Midwest at 79.1%, and every profiled hospital discloses at least some payer rates. CHI Health anchors the state with consistent performance, and Nebraska Medicine (UNMC) contributes strong academic disclosure.

Key Findings

  1. Every profiled hospital discloses payer rates — Nebraska is the only Midwest state with zero None-tier hospitals.
  2. CHI Health achieves consistent Full/Substantial performance across its Nebraska network.
  3. Nebraska Medicine (University of Nebraska Medical Center) provides strong academic hospital disclosure.
  4. 79.1% median exceeds every other Midwest state including North Dakota (76.7%) and South Dakota (76.8%).
  5. Only 19 of 92 non-VA hospitals profiled — good scores but limited coverage.
  6. Mercy presence in Nebraska scores Minimal — consistent with Mercy’s pattern across MO and KS.

About Transparency Tiers

Full ≥80% of items have payer-specific negotiated rates. Substantial 50–80%. Partial 20–50%. Minimal <20%. Transparency percentage measures the share of data rows containing a payer-specific negotiated rate — not just gross charges or cash prices.
