Ohio: Cleveland Clinic Discloses, OhioHealth Stays Dark

The Clinic averages 53% across 19 hospitals. OhioHealth’s 16 facilities remain at 0%.

67 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

Ohio’s transparency picture improved dramatically once Cleveland Clinic’s ZIP-compressed files were properly parsed. The Clinic averages 53% (Substantial) across 19 hospitals with 19–46 payers. TriHealth in Cincinnati hits 76–79%. But OhioHealth — Columbus’s dominant system — remains at 0%, and Kettering Health in Dayton scores 0.4–2.1%.

Key Findings

  1. Cleveland Clinic averages 53% transparency across 19 Ohio hospitals with 19–46 payers and a uniform 35% cash discount. The flagship Main Campus scores 38.5% — lower than its community hospitals.
  2. TriHealth (Cincinnati) is Ohio’s transparency leader: 76–79% at all 6 facilities with 114 payers and 25% cash discount.
  3. OhioHealth (16 Columbus-area hospitals) remains at 0% — files are valid JSON but the streaming profiler finds no negotiated dollar values. A structural disclosure gap.
  4. Kettering Health (9 Dayton-area hospitals) scores 0.4–2.1% — technically not zero, but functionally useless for price comparison.
  5. ProMedica and Aultman anchor northwest and northeast Ohio with consistent Full-tier performance.
  6. Cleveland Clinic Main Campus at 38.5% is a notable finding — the nation’s #2-ranked hospital discloses payer rates for barely a third of its services.

About Transparency Tiers

Full ≥80% of items have payer-specific negotiated rates. Substantial 50–80%. Partial 20–50%. Minimal <20%. Transparency percentage measures the share of data rows containing a payer-specific negotiated rate — not just gross charges or cash prices.
