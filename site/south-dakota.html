South Dakota: The Sanford-Avera Duopoly

Two systems cover the state. Both disclose. No gaps.

27 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

South Dakota’s hospital market is a duopoly: Sanford Health and Avera Health between them cover nearly every bed in the state. Both disclose consistently, producing a 76.8% median with zero None-tier hospitals.

Key Findings

  1. Avera Health and Sanford Health together account for nearly all 27 profiled hospitals — a true duopoly market.
  2. Zero None-tier hospitals — South Dakota joins Nebraska as a state where every profiled hospital discloses some payer rates.
  3. Monument Health (Rapid City) contributes additional coverage in western South Dakota.
  4. 76.8% median ranks third in the Midwest behind Nebraska (79.1%) and North Dakota (76.7%).
  5. The duopoly structure makes South Dakota unusually transparent — two standardized systems cover the entire state.
  6. 27 of 59 non-VA hospitals profiled — 46% discovery rate. Missing hospitals are likely small critical access facilities.
