Kansas: KU Health Leads, AdventHealth Pollutes the Data

7 Full-tier hospitals buried under 40 misattributed national entries.

48 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

Kansas has a data quality problem. AdventHealth’s national cms-hpt.txt dumps 38 non-Kansas facilities into our discovery pipeline — all scoring 0%. Strip those out and Kansas looks better: KU Health System achieves 100% across 5 facilities, and Stormont Vail in Topeka hits 80–86%.

Key Findings

  1. University of Kansas Health System achieves 100% transparency at all 5 facilities with 12–70 payers and cash discounts of 76–80%.
  2. Stormont Vail (Topeka) hits 80–86% at 2 facilities with 8 payers — solid regional performer.
  3. Hays Medical Center achieves 100% with 194 payers — one of the highest payer counts in the Midwest.
  4. 38 AdventHealth entries score 0% — these are Florida, Texas, and Colorado facilities pulled from AdventHealth’s national txt file, not Kansas hospitals.
  5. Only 2 actual Kansas-specific health systems discovered (KU, Stormont Vail) — the rest of the state’s 136 hospitals lack discoverable chargemasters.
  6. Mercy Hospital Pittsburg discloses only 1.4% of payer rates despite listing 22 payers — minimal compliance.

About Transparency Tiers

Full ≥80% of items have payer-specific negotiated rates. Substantial 50–80%. Partial 20–50%. Minimal <20%. Transparency percentage measures the share of data rows containing a payer-specific negotiated rate — not just gross charges or cash prices.
