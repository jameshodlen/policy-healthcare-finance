Illinois: The Academic Divergence

Northwestern and Rush comply fully. UChicago publishes almost nothing.

37 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

Three of Chicago's four academic flagships comply comprehensively. UChicago Medical Center discloses payer rates for only 2.9% of items — exclusively DRG bundles — while individual line items show only list prices.

Key Findings

  1. UChicago Medical Center discloses payer rates for 2.9% of items (2,302 of 78,504). Payer data exists only on DRG bundles; 97% of items show only gross charges and cash prices. Strategic selective disclosure.
  2. Northwestern Memorial Hospital achieves ~95% transparency with 32 payers across v3.0.0 JSON — comprehensive disclosure from Chicago's largest hospital.
  3. Rush University Medical Center hits 100% with 35 payers — proving Chicago's academic centers can comply fully.
  4. Advocate Health is remarkably uniform: all 11 facilities cluster at 60–70% with exactly 18 payers and a 50% cash discount. Centralized but incomplete.
  5. HSHS's Illinois hospitals (100%, 30–34 payers) outperform Ascension's Illinois presence (32–67%) — smaller system, better disclosure.
  6. Loyola Gottlieb offers an 81% cash discount — the highest in the three-state dataset — while disclosing only 56% of payer rates.

About Transparency Tiers
