Wisconsin: Where ThedaCare Leads and Froedtert Lags

The state with the highest median transparency — and the sharpest system-level contrasts.

31 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

ThedaCare achieves 100% transparency at all 9 facilities. Froedtert Hospital — Milwaukee's flagship — discloses rates for only 6 payers. UW Health discloses 112 payer contracts but remains on the outdated v2.0.0 schema.

Key Findings

  1. ThedaCare is the transparency gold standard: 100% payer rate disclosure at all 9 facilities, up to 59 payers, current v3.0.0 schema, updated March 2026.
  2. Froedtert Hospital — Milwaukee's largest, Level I Trauma Center — discloses rates for only 6 payers. A 25-bed critical access hospital in rural Boscobel (Gundersen) discloses more.
  3. UW Health discloses the most payer contracts in the state (112) using percentage-of-gross methodology, but its file is stale (January 2025) and still on v2.0.0 — at risk for CMS enforcement beginning April 2026.
  4. Ascension's three Wisconsin facilities are the least transparent (27–38%), consistent with national reporting on Ascension's disclosure approach.
  5. Marshfield's 5% cash discount is effectively no discount — a patient saves $50 on a $1,000 procedure. The lowest meaningful discount in the state.
  6. Gundersen's intra-system cash discount variance spans 21% to 62% across seven facilities — evidence of facility-level pricing autonomy.
