Missouri: BJC Shines, Mercy Hides, SSM Blocks

Three systems, three disclosure philosophies — and a polarized landscape.

84 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

Missouri’s 15.6% median masks a deeply polarized landscape. BJC Healthcare and CoxHealth achieve 100% at their flagship facilities. Mercy — Missouri’s largest Catholic system — shows 99% at small Stoddard County clinics but 1–16% at its major hospitals. SSM Health returns 0% across all entries.

Key Findings

  1. BJC Healthcare (Barnes-Jewish, St. Louis Children’s) achieves 92–100% across 13 facilities — comprehensive disclosure from Missouri’s premier academic system.
  2. CoxHealth hits 100% at all 4 Springfield-area facilities with 254–304 payers — extraordinary payer breadth.
  3. Mercy’s paradox: small rural clinics (Stoddard, Sikeston) disclose 98–99%, but major hospitals score Minimal (1–16%) or failed to download. Size correlates inversely with transparency.
  4. SSM Health returns 0% at all 21 entries — consistent with TLS/format blocking seen in Wisconsin and Illinois.
  5. Mercy rehab hospitals cluster at 5–14% with 0% cash discount — the lowest cash pricing transparency in the state.
  6. 34 Full-tier hospitals is misleading — many are small Mercy/CHI clinics. The major trauma centers are where the gaps are.

About Transparency Tiers

Full ≥80% of items have payer-specific negotiated rates. Substantial 50–80%. Partial 20–50%. Minimal <20%. Transparency percentage measures the share of data rows containing a payer-specific negotiated rate — not just gross charges or cash prices.
