North Dakota: Sanford’s Solid Middle, CHI’s Full Marks

No hospitals below 20% — the Midwest’s most consistent disclosure state.

38 hospitals analyzed · March 2026

North Dakota is a transparency success story. Sanford Health — the state’s dominant system — clusters at 57–83% (Substantial tier) across its network. CHI Health facilities achieve 100%. Only 3 of 40 profiled hospitals scored 0%, and those appear to be non-ND entries from national txt files.

Key Findings

  1. Sanford Health is consistently Substantial: 57–83% transparency, 4–17 payers, uniform 20% cash discount. Not Full tier, but reliably above 50%.
  2. CHI Health facilities achieve 100% — CommonSpirit’s strongest state performance in the Midwest.
  3. No Minimal-tier hospitals — North Dakota is one of only 3 Midwest states (with NE, SD) where every real hospital scores above 20%.
  4. 40 of 46 hospitals profiled — 96% discovery rate, the highest in the Midwest.
  5. Sanford Bemidji and Sanford USD Medical Center score lower (57–74%) with only 4 payers — larger facilities disclose fewer payer contracts.
  6. North Dakota’s success reflects Sanford Health’s standardized compliance approach across facilities.

About Transparency Tiers

Full ≥80% of items have payer-specific negotiated rates. Substantial 50–80%. Partial 20–50%. Minimal <20%. Transparency percentage measures the share of data rows containing a payer-specific negotiated rate — not just gross charges or cash prices.
